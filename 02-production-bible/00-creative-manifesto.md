# Creative Manifesto

> 我们不去预感未来，只记录当下。

We don't predict the future.

We simply record the present.

---

## What This Is

This is not a screenplay.

It is the compass for every creative decision in The Trail Project.

The film should not be written to impress.

It should be shaped by what is true.

---

## Record the Present

The film is built from moments honestly experienced, not moments created to impress.

If a scene exists only because it sounds beautiful, we question it.

If a scene exists because it feels true, we protect it.

---

## Trust the Truth

When reality is beautiful enough, don't improve it.

Trust the truth.

---

## The West Coast Trail Is Our Co-writer

The trail is not a setting.

It is an active storyteller.

Ladders.
Mud.
Tides.
Rain.
Silence.
Fatigue.
Waiting.
Beauty.

The trail provides the drama.

We should never invent drama when the trail already provides it.

---

## The Journey Begins With Yes

The first important step is not the first ladder.

It is replying:

> Yes.

---

## Richard Is Not Escaping Life

Richard enjoys his work.

He enjoys building gardens.

He enjoys ordinary life.

He is not running away.

He is finally saying yes to something that has waited for years.

---

## Show, Never Explain

Characters never explain the meaning of the trail.

Meaning belongs to the audience.

---

## Silence Is Dialogue

Wind.
Ocean.
Footsteps.
Rain.
Breathing.

Silence is never empty.

---

## We Are Filming What Stayed

The film is not about what happened.

It is about what remained after returning home.

Memories that quietly return while driving, working, hearing waves, or smelling cedar.

---

## Protect the Experience

Do not chase emotion.

Do not chase drama.

Protect the experience.

---

## One Question

Every scene should answer one question:

> Is this true?

If not, rewrite it.
