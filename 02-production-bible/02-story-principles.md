# Story Principles

## The Journey Begins With Yes

Some journeys begin with the first step.

This one begins with **Yes**.

Richard has wanted to hike the West Coast Trail for years.

Every year, life gave him a reason to postpone it:

- no suitable partner
- no long-distance backpacking experience
- difficult permit reservation
- work commitments
- ordinary life

The film begins when an old hiking friend sends a simple message:

> Someone dropped out.
>
> Want the spot?

Richard does not make a speech.

He does not explain his life.

He simply replies:

> Yes.

That word is the true first step of the film.

---

## Not a Tragedy

The journey begins with an invitation, not a tragedy.

This separates **The Trail** from stories about walking in response to death or loss.

Richard is not completing another person's pilgrimage.

He is finally saying yes to something he has postponed for years.

---

## Inspired by Real Memories

The story is inspired by real memories, not constrained by them.

The West Coast Trail experience must feel authentic.

Characters may be fictionalized.

Names, details, and life situations can change.

But the emotional truth must remain.

---

## The West Coast Trail Is the Co-writer

The West Coast Trail is not our setting.

It is our co-writer.

The trail drives the story through:

- ladders
- mud
- tides
- boulders
- weather
- camp life
- fatigue
- beauty
- the long walk out

Never invent drama when the trail already provides it.

---

## The Standard

If a real WCT hiker says:

> That's not how it feels.

We rewrite it.
