# What This Film Is Not

## The Trail is NOT a philosophy film.

It is a human story that happens on the West Coast Trail.

The philosophy should never lead the story.
The story should naturally reveal the philosophy.

## The mountain is not a metaphor.

The West Coast Trail is a real place.
Its ladders, beaches, mud, rain, campsites and ocean are not symbols.
They shape the characters because they are real.

## The audience should fall in love with the people first.

Only after the credits may they discover that the film quietly changed how they see life.

## Creative Rule

Never stop the story to explain an idea.
Let the audience discover it themselves.

Our goal is simple:

Tell one unforgettable story about ordinary people who spend six days walking the West Coast Trail together.

Everything else is secondary.
