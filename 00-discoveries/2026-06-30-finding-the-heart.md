# Finding the Heart

Date: 2026-06-30

Today we stopped trying to write a better movie.

Instead, we tried to understand why this movie should exist.

## Discoveries

### D-001 — The West Coast Trail is our co-writer

The West Coast Trail is not our setting.

It is our co-writer.

The trail drives the story through ladders, mud, tides, rain, fatigue, waiting, beauty, and the long walk out.

### D-002 — The journey begins with Yes

Some journeys begin with the first step.

This one begins with **Yes**.

The hardest step on the West Coast Trail was not the first ladder.

It was replying:

> Yes.

### D-003 — Richard is not escaping life

Richard is not running away from ordinary life.

He likes his work.
He likes gardens.
He likes being useful.

The West Coast Trail is not an escape.

It is a part of life that waited long enough.

### D-004 — We record the present

> 我们不去预感未来，只记录当下。

We do not predict the future.

We record the present.

### D-005 — We are filming what stayed

The film is not about what happened.

It is about what remained after returning home.

The trail does not end at Pachena.

It quietly returns in ordinary life.

### D-006 — Trust the truth

When reality is beautiful enough, don't improve it.

Trust the truth.

### D-007 — Never invent drama when the trail already provides it

Tide.
Ladders.
Mud.
Rain.
Fatigue.
Waiting.
Silence.

The trail already provides drama.

### D-008 — Silence is dialogue

Wind.
Ocean.
Footsteps.
Rain.
Breathing.

Silence is never empty.

### D-009 — Meaning belongs to the audience

Characters should not explain what the trail means.

The audience should discover meaning by themselves.

### D-010 — Protect the experience

Do not chase emotion.

Do not chase drama.

Protect the experience.

## Creative Reminder

Do not film the whole trail.

Film the moments that never leave.
