# D-016 — Narrative Inspired by 'The Mountain, The Man, The Dog'

## Core Discovery

The Trail should not imitate the plot of the film, but its narrative philosophy.

The trail is not merely a backdrop.
It is the space where people gradually reveal themselves.

## Narrative Principles

- Use the trail as the timeline.
- Use camps as chapters.
- Use encounters as stories.
- Let relationships emerge naturally instead of relying on dramatic twists.

## Camp as Stage

Each camp becomes a temporary stage where people from different directions and different lives briefly meet.

Stories begin at camp and continue on the trail.

## Annual Structure

Each year's documentary follows different hikers, but the camps remain constant.

The places stay.
The people change.

Over time this becomes a living archive of the West Coast Trail.

## Guiding Sentence

"Use the trail as time, the camps as chapters, and encounters as the story."
