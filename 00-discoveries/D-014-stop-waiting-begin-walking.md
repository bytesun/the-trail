# D-014 — Stop Waiting. Begin Walking

## Discovery

The central conflict of *The Trail* is not whether Richard can finish the West Coast Trail.

The real conflict is:

**When does he stop waiting and finally begin?**

The West Coast Trail is not the story.

Waiting is the story.

The trail is the place where waiting ends.

---

## Three-Act Structure

### Act I — Waiting
A dream lives quietly inside an ordinary life for many years.

### Act II — Walking
The West Coast Trail provides the conflict. Nature provides the drama. The characters provide the emotion.

### Act III — Living
The destination is not Pachena.
The destination is returning to ordinary life with a dream finally fulfilled.

---

## Principle

Do not ask:

"What happened on the trail?"

Ask:

"What happened to Richard?"

Because every scene should ultimately serve the human journey.

---

## Working Tagline

Some trails are measured in miles.
The most important one is measured in years.
